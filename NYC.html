About The Big Apple

New York City is made up of five boroughs which include Queens, Manhattan, Brooklyn, the Bronx, and Staten Island. The city is the home of approximately 8 million people. In 1876, France gifted the City of New York what is known as the Statue of Liberty, which is currently located on Liberty Island and commonly visited by tourists. Another tourist destination is Times Square. Times Square is commonly known for the big buildings, Broadway shows, and bright neon signs. This famous location was named after The New York Times after the Times moved to that location. Prior to that, it was named Longacre Square.

New York City is also known for its iconic bridges that connect the boroughs and allow ease of transportation across the vast metropolis.

NYC Foodie Guide

New York City is very popular for the variety of great food it has. Some of the top food items in NYC include:

  1. Pizza (especially New York style!)
  2. Bagels with lox and cream cheese
  3. Classic Burgers and Deli Sandwiches
  4. Authentic Ramen bowls
  5. Street Tacos from diverse vendors
  6. Delicious Pasta dishes
  7. Iconic Desserts like cheesecake
